## Welcome to tag.dev

I finally did it, I created my own blog that hopefully serves as my own portfolio/webpage, or as a digital footprint that is forever going to curse me. Funny how trying out OpenClaude led me to create the thing I told myself 3 years ago that I would do, and what I also told my professors back at uni. Funnily enough, its here.

Was it out of boredom? My current job its clearly getting messy and I cannot help but remember one quote from "Facts & Fallacies From Software Engineering": _A good programmer is up to 20x better than a bad one_. I've never thought this was the case, maybe because of how capable the members of my current team are, but working in the AI Transition has proven it to me. I personally don't feel that I'm that good, but sometimes it feels like some people just don't even try.

If there's one thing I despise about some programmers in the AI era, is closing their eyes. Sure, AI is the shiny new beatiful rock that all the investors are thristy about, but is it really good enough for us to let our hands off the keyboard and walk through the path of replacement? Why is it that after trying 20+ times, and failing, people are still trying again without modifying their approach?

Those are the questions that fuel my frustration, and what led me to create this page.

### What to expect from this blog

- **Ocassional Venting**: Pretty much like this first blog post.
- **Random experiments**: Because that's how we learn and Astro islands feel very cool.
- **Straightforward and friendly writing**: Even though I plan at some point to convert this page into something I can be proud of, I'm not going to get bored while writing it.
- **Deep dives**: This will be my ideal place to learn about stuff, and have somewhere to come back to when I need said info.
